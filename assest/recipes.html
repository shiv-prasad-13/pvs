     Chicken biryani

       Chicken biryani is a cherished Indian culinary masterpiece, a symphony      of flavors and aromas that captivates the senses. This delectable dish     combines tender chicken pieces with fragrant Basmati rice, melding         together in a harmonious blend of spices and herbs.

      Chicken biryani is more than just a meal; it's a celebration of flavors, a      testament to the rich culinary heritage of India, and a dish that unites        people with its irresistible appeal. With every mouthful, it tells a story of        tradition, love, and the joy of savoring life's delicious moments.


                                                        List of ingredients:

  1. For Marinating the Chicken:

    • 500 grams of chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 cup of yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

  2. For the Rice:

    • 2 cups of long-grain Basmati rice
    • Water for soaking rice
    • 4-5 cups of water for cooking rice
    • 2-3 green cardamom pods
    • 2-3 cloves
    • 1-inch cinnamon stick
    • Salt to taste

  1. For Biryani Masala:

    • 2 onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 green chilies, slit
    • 1/2 cup of chopped mint leaves
    • 1/2 cup of chopped coriander leaves
    • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)

  2. For Layering:

    • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (for coloring)
    • Fried onions (from the sliced onions used in masala)
    • Chopped mint and coriander leaves
    • Ghee (clarified butter)

                                                                Instructions:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:

    • In a bowl, mix together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and vegetable oil.
    • Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade.
    • Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator.

  2. Preparing the Rice:

    • Wash the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, bring 4-5 cups of water to a boil.
    • Add the soaked and drained rice along with green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon, and salt.
    • Cook the rice until it's 70% cooked (it should still have a slight bite). Drain the rice and set it aside.

  3. Biryani Masala:

    • Heat vegetable oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or pot.
    • Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove some fried onions and set them aside for garnishing.
    • Add green chilies, chopped tomatoes, and cook until the tomatoes turn soft and the oil separates from the mixture.
    • Add the marinated chicken and cook until it's no longer pink and starts to brown.
    • Stir in chopped mint and coriander leaves.

  4. Layering and Dum Cooking:

    • In a large heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice.
    • Top it with the chicken masala mixture.
    • Sprinkle some saffron milk, fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves.
    • Add the remaining rice as the top layer.
    • Drizzle ghee over the top layer.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and the flavors meld together.li>

  5. Serve:

    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
    • Serve hot with raita or a side salad.

          Chicken fried rice

      Chicken fried rice is the comfort dish of Chinese food. It's been around       for a very long time and is also common in East, Southeast, and South      Asian cuisines. The dish started as way to use leftover fried rice that     has dried out some and may not be great on its own, but is still     perfectly edible. The addition of oil and soy sauce and veggies and       meat spruce up the rice for a delicious meal. A traditional Chinese fried       rice would be made in a wok over high heat, but we've made ours in a     cast iron skilletnbspsince they are more common in home kitchens.       This take on the favorite is easy to make and makes the perfect lunch or       dinner.

                                                        List of ingredients:

  1. For the Chicken Marinade:

    • 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast or thigh, diced into small pieces
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
    • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon ginger powder
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  1. For the Fried Rice:

    • 2 cups cooked and cooled white rice (preferably leftover rice)
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 1/2 cup diced carrots
    • 1/2 cup frozen peas
    • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
    • 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce (adjust to taste)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

                                                                Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    • In a bowl, combine the diced chicken with soy sauce, cornstarch, garlic powder, ginger powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Cook the Chicken:

    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    • Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it's cooked through and slightly browned. Remove the cooked chicken from the pan and set it aside.

  3. Prepare the Rice:

    • In the same pan, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil if needed.
    • Add minced garlic and chopped onions. Sauté until they become fragrant and the onions turn translucent.

  4. Stir-fry the Vegetables:

    • Add the diced carrots and frozen peas to the pan. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

  5. Scramble the Eggs:

    • Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and pour the lightly beaten eggs into the other side.
    • Quickly scramble the eggs with a spatula until they are cooked but still slightly runny.

  6. Combine Ingredients:

    • Add the cooked rice and previously cooked chicken back into the pan with the vegetables and eggs.
    • Drizzle soy sauce over the mixture and stir-fry everything together, ensuring that the ingredients are well combined.li>

  7. Season and Garnish:

    • Season the fried rice with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the amount of soy sauce if needed.
    • Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.

  8. Serve:

    • Transfer the chicken fried rice to serving plates or a large serving dish.
    • Serve hot and enjoy your homemade chicken fried rice.




     Green Chicken

    Hariyali chicken or green chicken is a delicious Indian dish made       with chicken, fresh aromatic herbs and spices. It goes well with plain      rice, jeera rice, roti or even with ghee rice. There are a lot of Indian     dishes that are cooked with a green paste made with green chilli,        coriander and mint leaves.

                                                        List of ingredients:

  1. For the Green Curry Paste:

    • 2-3 green Thai chili peppers (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 2-3 cloves garlic
    • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
    • 1 lemongrass stalk, white part only, thinly sliced
    • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
    • 1 bunch of fresh cilantro, stems and leaves separated
    • 1 bunch of fresh basil leaves
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • Zest and juice of 1 lime
    • Salt to taste

     

  1. For the Green Chicken Curry:

    • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 can (13.5 ounces) of coconut milk
    • 1-2 tablespoons green curry paste (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
    • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
    • 1 cup sliced bamboo shoots (optional)
    • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • Fresh basil leaves and cilantro leaves for garnish
    • Cooked jasmine rice for serving

                                                                Instructions:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:
